Olivia Rodrigo Biography

She was born on 20 February 2003,

She was born in a well-settled family from Murrieta, California, where she spent her childhood. She went to Lisa J. Mails Elementary School and Dorothy McElhinney Middle School for her early schooling in Murrieta, California. When she bagged the role for ‘Bizaardvark’, she moved to Los Angeles from Murrieta.

At age twelve, she made her first screen appearance in an “Old Navy” TV commercial in 2015. Later that year, she debuted in the film “An American Girl: Grace Stirs Up Success”. She slowly started getting recognized due to her roles in “Bizaardvark” (2016) and “New Girl” (2017).

In April 2022, Olivia Rodrigo won three Grammy Awards for her song “drivers license” and album “Sour”. She is also a recipient of the Brits Award 2022 for her single “good 4 u” and several other awards for her recent works. In total, she is a recipient of 20 awards and 15 nominations.

Aged 19 years (as of July 2022), Olivia Rodrigo has lately become a famous pop sensation around the world and has gained much fame and success in the early years of her career. As of July 2022, she has over 27.9 million followers on Instagram and 1.8 million followers on Twitter.

Having a knack for singing since early childhood, Rodrigo believes that “there is nothing that is a truer window into human emotion than music.”

Olivia Rodrigo Career

Olivia Rodrigo started her career as an actress in a commercial for Old Navy. In the year 2015, she did the role of Grace Thomas in the video film ‘An American Girl: Grace Stirs Up Success’.

In 2019, she came into the spotlight thanks to a leading role in the Disney+ original series “High School Musical: The Musical: The Series”. Her popularity skyrocketed with the release of her debut solo song, “drivers license”, which broke several records and went to the top of global charts within a few days of its release in January 2021.

In February 2021, she appeared on the popular television talk show “Saturday Night Live” as a musical guest.

After the chart-busting success of “drivers license”, Rodrigo came up with two subsequent singles, “deja vu” and “good 4 u,” in April and May of the same year before releasing her debut album “Sour” in May 2021 to critical and commercial acclaim.
