Emily Blunt Is Royally Elegant in an Ivory Cape Blazer and Gold Accents

From gilded gowns to sleek jumpsuits to power suits, Emily Blunt has been stepping out in back-to-back dreamy looks, ensuring no one forgets her red carpet royalty status for even a second.

Michael Kovac

For her latest appearance, the Oppenheimer actor made an appearance at the AFI Awards Luncheon in Los Angeles on Friday, looking simply gorgeous and ultra-elegant in a structured cape blazer in an ivory hue. The piece featured a plunging neckline, peak lapels, two rows of decorative white buttons, one button that fastened it all together, and a square construction that formed a point at the hem. Blunt paired the sophisticated businesswear-inspired top with creamy white cigarette trousers that featured front pleats, slightly sheer beige pointed-toe ballet flats, and a boxy bright white clutch with rose gold hardware. She accented the charming ensemble with oversize gold statement earrings, a few gold rings, and a dainty, barely visible chain necklace. Blunt opted for pretty, glowy glam in the form of a nude pink lip, light bronze eye shadow, and a dewy highlight, while sweeping her hair back into a lovely slicked-back ponytail.

The Devil Wears Prada actor traded elevated office chic for Hollywood glamour—but looked just as regal—at the 2024 Golden Globes. She graced the carpet like an ethereal vision in a gilded gown with a sheer white tulle skirt, custom-made by Alexander McQueen. The breathtaking piece featured thin straps, a subtle V neck, a fitted bodice adorned with gold sequins in an abstract floral-like embroidery, and a sweeping skirt that hid her Jimmy Choo shoes.

Blunt was nominated for a Globe for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture for her role in Oppenheimer; she ultimately lost to Da’Vine Joy Randolph, who won for her performance in The Holdovers. Blunt will continue her award show streak tomorrow, Sunday, January 14, at the Critics Choice Awards, where she is once again up for Best Supporting Actress for her role in Oppenheimer.
