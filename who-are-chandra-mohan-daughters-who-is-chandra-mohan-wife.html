Who was Chandra Mohan?

Actor Chandra Mohan was primarily recognized for his roles in Telugu movies. He received two Nandi Awards and a Filmfare Award South. On May 23, 1945, Chandra Mohan was born Mallampalli Chandrasekhara Rao in the Andhra Pradesh village of Pamidimukkala in the Krishna district. 

He attended Y.V.R.M.Z.P. High School in Meduru for his education. He received his degree from Bapatla’s Agriculture College. He was K. Viswanath’s (the veteran filmmaker) cousin. Rangula Ratnam, released in 1966, served as Chandra Mohan’s introduction to the Telugu cinema industry. As the devoted brother of Vanisri’s character in Sukha Duhkalu (1968), he won awards for the role.

He won the state Nandi Award for Best Actor for Rangula Ratnam (1966), the Filmfare Best Actor Award (Telugu) for Padaharella Vayasu (1978), and the Siri Siri Muvva (1978) box office hits, among others, garnered positive reviews for him. Naalai Namadhe, his debut Tamil film, came out in 1975.

He acted as the lead in several movies, including Chandamama Raave (1987), Seetamalakshmi (1978), Ram Robert Rahim (1980), Radha Kalyanam (1981), and Rendu Rellu Aaru (1986).

Chandra Mohan Career

In 1966, Chandra Mohan made his debut in the Telugu film industry with the release of “Rangula Ratnam”. His acting career took off, and he starred in the 1968 film “Sukha Duhkalu,” playing the devoted brother of Vanisri, the actress. He began a successful career and received awards for this role.

Chandra Mohan gained praise from critics for his roles in a number of successful box office films over the course of his career. He won the state Nandi Award for Best Actor for “Rangula Ratnam” (1966), the Filmfare Best Actor Award in Telugu for “Padaharella Vayasu” (1978), and the title of “Siri Siri Muvva” (1978). These three films are particularly noteworthy. In addition, he made his cinematic debut in Tamil with “Naalai Namadhe” (1975).

Chandra Mohan demonstrated his versatility as an actor by starring in several films. “Seetamalakshmi” (1978), “Ram Robert Rahim” (1980), “Radha Kalyanam” (1981), “Rendu Rellu Aaru” (1986), and “Chandamama Raave” (1987) are one or more of these movies. His performances in a variety of roles and his contributions to the film industry made him a well-respected character in Telugu cinema.
